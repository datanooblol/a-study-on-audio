04 · Feature Extraction & Audio Embeddings

The bridge between "cleaned audio" and what a model actually consumes.

Overview

A cleaned waveform is still just a long list of numbers. Almost no model — classical or neural — learns directly from raw samples at 16,000 numbers per second. Instead, audio gets converted into a more compact, informative representation first: a hand-crafted feature like a mel-spectrogram or MFCC, or a learned embedding from a pretrained model. Every task later in this curriculum — transcription, diarization, verification, emotion recognition — starts from one of a small handful of these representations.

Key concepts

Mel-spectrogram

Start from the STFT spectrogram (lesson 01). Human pitch perception isn't linear — we distinguish low frequencies far more finely than high ones. The mel scale reshapes the frequency axis to match that perception, via a bank of triangular filters (a mel filterbank) that are narrow and closely spaced at low frequencies and wide and sparse at high ones. The result, a mel-spectrogram, is the default input/output representation for most modern speech and TTS models.

MFCCs (Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients)

Take the log of a mel-spectrogram, then apply a Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) to decorrelate it into a compact set of coefficients — typically 13 to 40 per frame, versus dozens of mel bands. MFCCs were the default feature for decades of classical speech and speaker recognition systems (GMM-HMM era) and remain a fast, effective baseline feature today.

Chroma & pitch (F0)

  • Chroma features fold spectral energy into the 12 pitch classes of the musical octave (C, C♯, D, …) — mainly used for music analysis (key, chord detection), less so for speech.
  • Pitch / F0 (fundamental frequency) tracks the perceived pitch of a voice over time — central to prosody, emotion, and TTS naturalness.

Hand-crafted features vs. learned embeddings

Hand-crafted (mel-spectrogram, MFCC, chroma)

Computed by a fixed mathematical formula. Fast, interpretable, no training required — but they encode a human's assumptions about what matters, not what a specific task actually needs.

Learned embeddings (wav2vec2, HuBERT, WavLM, CLAP)

A neural network pretrained on huge amounts of unlabeled audio produces contextual vector representations — the audio equivalent of BERT/GPT embeddings for text. Fine-tune a small head on top for a specific task instead of engineering features by hand.

The mel-spectrogram path feeds classical pipelines and most TTS vocoders. The learned-embedding path feeds most modern ASR, diarization, verification, and emotion models — often the same pretrained encoder is reused across all of them, just with a different small head trained on top.

A little history

  • 1980 — MFCCsDavis & Mermelstein introduce Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients, which become the default feature for speech recognition for the next three decades.
  • ~2012 — Neural acoustic modelsDNN-HMM hybrid systems start replacing GMMs as the classifier on top of MFCC/mel features, the first big deep learning win in ASR.
  • 2019–2021 — Self-supervised learningwav2vec, wav2vec2, and HuBERT show that pretraining directly on raw audio (no labels) produces representations that transfer across many audio tasks, the same way BERT/GPT did for text.
  • 2022+ — Audio-text joint embeddingsModels like CLAP learn a shared embedding space for audio and text, enabling zero-shot audio classification and retrieval.

Evaluation

n/a directly — features and embeddings are judged indirectly by how well the downstream task performs (each later lesson's own metric). Benchmarks like SUPERB exist specifically to compare learned embeddings across many downstream tasks at once.
