11 · Composing an Audio AI Pipeline

No new technique here — just putting lessons 01–10 together, and being honest about how their errors compound.

Overview

Real audio products are rarely a single model. A meeting-transcription tool needs preprocessing, ASR, and diarization, merged together. A call-center analytics product adds speaker verification and emotion tracking on top of that. This lesson maps real use cases onto the modules that solve each piece, and closes with what phase 2 (code) will actually build first.

Where errors compound

Pipeline error is a product, not a sum

Chaining a 95%-accurate diarization step with a 95%-accurate ASR step does not give a 95%-accurate combined transcript. Errors compound: a small timing misalignment between diarization and ASR misattributes words right at every speaker-change boundary; ASR substitution errors corrupt any downstream text-based emotion or sentiment fusion; preprocessing artifacts (lesson 03) ripple into every stage built on top of them. Composing a pipeline means budgeting for this — measuring the combined output, not just each component in isolation.

A real evaluation metric for exactly this problem exists: cpWER (concatenated minimum-permutation Word Error Rate), used in the CHiME speech-recognition challenges, concatenates each speaker's words (trying every speaker-label permutation) and computes WER on the result — scoring the ASR-plus-diarization pipeline as a whole, rather than each piece separately.

Tools & orchestration

No new libraries here — a composed pipeline reuses the tools from every earlier lesson (Whisper/NeMo for ASR, pyannote for diarization, speechbrain for verification/emotion, Coqui/VITS for TTS) glued together by whatever orchestration code phase 2 builds.

Evaluation

There's no single pipeline-wide metric — it's task-specific. For meeting transcription, cpWER (above) is the closest thing to a standard. For a voice assistant, end-to-end task success rate (did the user get what they asked for) matters more than any individual component's score.
